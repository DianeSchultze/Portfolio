Shangri-La Beach Resort

A luxury beach resort website reimagined for the modern traveller

GitHub Repo Live Site

The Problem

The original Shangri-La Beach Resort website did not reflect the luxury and atmosphere of the destination. The visual direction felt dated, and the structure made it difficult for users to engage with the content or navigate with intention.

The Solution

A full redesign focused on clarity, visual hierarchy, and a more refined aesthetic. The experience was rebuilt to feel calm, elevated, and aligned with a high-end travel brand.

Tech Stack

  • HTML5 for semantic structure
  • CSS3 for layout and styling
  • JavaScript for interactivity

Key Features

  • Image-driven layout
  • Responsive design
  • Improved content flow

Process

This project was completed during the second semester at SAIT for the Intermediate Web Communications course under Dan Stephenson. The focus was on translating a physical experience into a digital one.

Design decisions centered around spacing, typography, and imagery. Times New Roman Italic was used for headings to introduce a sense of elegance, paired with Inter for readability. Layout and hierarchy were carefully structured to guide users naturally through the content.

Outcome

The final result presents a more modern and cohesive experience that better reflects the resort’s identity. The redesign improves usability while creating a stronger emotional connection through its visual direction.
