Granted

A scholarship platform designed to make opportunities across Alberta easier to browse and save

Live Site

The Problem

Searching for scholarships can feel overwhelming for students, especially when information is scattered, deadlines are hard to track, and opportunities are not presented in a clear way. The goal was to build a platform that made real scholarships around Alberta easier to explore and manage.

The Solution

Granted was developed as a scholarship platform built with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL. The experience focused on improving clarity, browsing, filtering, and saving opportunities while keeping the interface approachable and student-friendly.

Tech Stack

  • HTML5 and CSS3 for structure and styling
  • JavaScript for interaction and interface behavior
  • PHP for server-side logic
  • MySQL for database design and data storage

Key Features

  • Scholarship browsing and discovery
  • Filtering and sorting options
  • Saved scholarship functionality
  • Database-driven platform structure

Process

Granted was a capstone group project completed with Michelle Babani, Ivy Porras, Chris Nordrum, Mharvic Inocentes, and Kevin Mai. The project focused on creating a functional scholarship platform featuring real scholarship opportunities from across Alberta, Canada.

My role centered on front-end development, including developing the high-fidelity wireframes into the final interface. I had to take over in the front-end developer role, which meant translating the visual direction into a working product while also refining layout behavior, styling consistency, and overall user experience.

A major part of the project involved balancing practical functionality with a design that felt calm and accessible. Because scholarship platforms can easily become dense or overwhelming, the interface was designed to support browsing, filtering, and saving opportunities in a way that felt more organized and approachable.

Outcome

The final result is a database-backed scholarship platform that combines useful functionality with a more thoughtful front-end experience. It demonstrates my ability to step into a key front-end role, develop from high-fidelity wireframes, and contribute to a collaborative capstone project with real content and practical user needs.
